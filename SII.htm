2024-063_F04 SII 2024 2024-063 Technology Governance (DBAG, ECAG, CBF, CBL, CFCL) 2024-063_F04-A01 SII Action Plan Open S3 Lack of standardised framework around data management and data governance Chief Technology Officer (ZJS) reported that DBAG has established basic data governance and management framework in 2021, including roles and responsibilities, guidelines, processes and a catalog tool (DBG Data Catalog [AID1040] - Microsoft Purview). The framework was already partially rolled out in the Group (only to parts of Clearstream entities). DBAG also launched a data catalog tool, fitting to the earlier organisation strategic goals. However, the roles and responsibilities were not fully aligned with the DBG existing roles (i.e. Information ownership, etc.). The situation led to de-centralised, non-standardised or no standardised data governance within the Group. Additionally, the earlier introduced data catalogue tool did not sufficiently support the latest DBG Hyperion program strategy focusing on Data Mesh  implementation across DBG. This resulted into a lack of a common data inventory, without transparency to data ownership and data quality accountabilities across the Group.

With Hyperion’s Data Mesh strategy, the data governance and management framework need update, including the launch of an efficient Data Catalog tool solution for the Group.

The new strategic requirements are included in the Hyperion Layer 2 Project and the resulting distributed data architecture, with a federated data governance framework from Data Mesh as well as alignment with the existing information ownership roles.

Since 2023, DBAG has made progress towards the targets as follows:
§ Aligned the data management and governance framework as per Data Mesh, currently running alignment of this framework with information ownership roles, with DBAG CISOs’ involvement.
§ Conducted a request for proposal (RfP), in cooperation with all legal entities’ (LEs) relevant stakeholders, to select the Group’s appropriate data catalog solution, finalised the tool and currently onboarding the tool to DBG infrastructure.
§ Has drafted a Data Governance Policy, covering minimum requirements to be fulfilled by LEs for establishing a federated DBG data governance framework.

Root Cause: The root cause was attributed to a heterogenous data management governance structure across the DBG LEs.

Risk: Lack of standardised and federated framework around data management and governance may lead to inadequate governance and structure of data, lack of data discoverability in the Group and poor data quality due to missing accountability and data ownership. This may expose the Group to a major financial impact, serious reputational damage, due to public sanctions, and serious gaps in the internal and external provisions.		 Intended actions: The standardised federated data governance framework shall be implemented in the LEs. An action plan was shared and is supported by regular Steering Committee progress tracking in the Hyperion Layer 2 project initiative. The action plan needs to be supported by LEs’ business and relevant stakeholders. The following action plan will be implemented for DBAG in alignment with local legal requirements:
§ Align roles and responsibilities, already defined in previous years, with the information ownership roles. LEs’ relevant business and IT stakeholders to support alignment and adoption.
§ Finalise and launch the Group’s Data Governance Policy, Standards and relevant guidelines -already reviewed by DBAG Policy Approval Committee. The formal review process started in Q3 2024, is still ongoing, and will reach all LE Executive Boards for approval. DBAG to collect all LE Executive Board decisions; where LEs adopted the policy, the respective LE coordinator should produce the evidence of policy implementation at the end of an agreed transition period.
§ Introduce Enterprise Data Catalog tool Atlan (AID2206) and develop respective KPIs - the Group solution has been selected with a completed RfP coordinated with LEs (Clearstream, Eurex, EEX, ISS, DBAG), and will be validated by local MCPs (Material Change Process). Currently, the tool is in onboarding phase and will be live in phases until Q1 2025.
§ Started in September 2024, the tool launch will continue for all Group entities over 2025. Onboarding will continue as per priority and planning with individual LE business owners over 2025-26.

2025-001_F01 SII 2025 2025-001 IT Operation Management (DBAG, ECAG, CBF, CBL, CFCL) 2025-001_F01-A01 SII Action Plan Open S3 Inadequate Cloud container governance and security Containers are an abstraction layer used in Cloud computing. They are lightweight packages of application code bundled with all its dependent elements, therefore allowing flexible deployment, shifting and running in any (Cloud or on-premises) environment. In contrast to virtual machines (VMs), containers do not encompass an underlying operating system and are therefore easier to manage in terms of size, portability and operations.

As containers introduce a new layer between operating systems and applications, the IT governance framework would also need specific requirements and processes to ensure their secure management and operations.

Chief Cloud Officer / Core Infrastructure (ZFJ) and IT Governance, Risk and Transformation (IRT) controls reviews in January 2025 noted a lack of defined and documented IT and IS Governance requirements for Cloud container asset types and container platforms (scope: OpenShift, Kubernetes). As a result, non-standardised and de-centralised heterogeneous container technologies and containerisation practices have evolved and led to several deficiencies across the control environment:
* Undefined roles and responsibilities
* Asset management: lack of clear ownership of each container, as well as missing linkage with applications and business processes
* Change management & Privileged Access Management: gaps and manual practices in the change processes resulting in the need for overprivileged developer access to production containers (particularly OpenShift) for infrastructure provisioning
* Security: OpenShift platforms not under governance of the “Secure Landing Zones” (SLZ), which were designed for cloud-native workloads in Google Cloud Platform (GCP) and Microsoft (MS) Azure; inability to perform software scans inside container images with DBG’s discovery solutions

This impacted all legal entities using GCP and MS Azure and relying on OpenShift.

Due to the thematic overlap, the aspects covered in this issue were merged from three separate SIIs on Cloud container governance.		 In alignment with the different parties involved, the responsible action owners have proactively started to address and continue the implementation around the Cloud and Secure Landing Zones across the DBG LEs. High-level responsibilities are as follows with owners taking the lead to complete:

** Supported by CTO and in coordination with Group Security (GS), IT Governance, Risk and Transformation (IRT) to:
- review and improve the governance around container asset type, reflected in the ICT Asset and Change Management framework.
- enhance the baselines for approved container technologies and configurations.
Resolution due date: 31 January 2026

** IRT in coordination with GS,
- perform a proof of concept using PA CNAPP - Palo Alto’s Cloud-Native Application Protection Platform (AID2146) - sourced discovery data to enrich container asset information in DBG’s CMS system.
- depending on the implementation of the IT Governance requirements, assess and implement a discovery tool supporting DBG container technologies.
Resolution due date: 15 April 2026

** Chief Cloud Officer / Core Infrastructure (ZFJ) to adapt the Azure and GCP landing zone for OpenShift workloads i.e. by:
- designing and documenting the desired target state.
- implementing automated controls for OpenShift platforms on SLZs.
- mitigate risks of high privileges required for installation and configuration.
Resolution due date: 15 July 2026
